Avid CNC Lean Trike

Summer 2019, I was invited to work with the engineers at Avid CNC, a company that makes CNC routers and plasma kits. My project: Build a DIY style electric vehicle Avid CNC could use to demonstrate the types of intricate, fun projects you could make using their CNC machines. To accomplish this my idea was to make an electric tricycle with two front wheels, full suspension, linkage steering, and leaning capabilities.

I fabricated all the custom parts for this vehicle using the CNC routers and CNC plasma machines at Avid CNC headquarters in North Bend, Washington.

I had never designed a bike like this from scratch so I had to do a lot of research on suspension geometry, steering geometry, and bike frame design. The end product was riddles with minor design mistakes and I made a pretty critical structural design flaw but overall, I think I definitely accomplished the goal of making a fun, eye-catching project to show off.
